## Welcome to the webcam.org Community

webcam.org is a platform for sharing public webcam views and self-hosted security cameras. Our community is built on **privacy, respect, and responsible sharing**.

These guidelines help ensure a safe, legal, and positive experience for everyone.

## ‚ùå What You CANNOT Share

### Prohibited Content

**‚úó Private Spaces**
- Bathrooms, bedrooms, changing rooms
- Indoor areas without proper signage and business justification
- Private areas of homes (yours or others')
- Hospital rooms, therapy offices, private medical spaces

**‚úó Others' Private Property**
- Neighbors' windows, backyards, or driveways
- Anyone else's home without explicit written permission
- Private businesses without owner consent

**‚úó Deceptive or Malicious**
- Fake locations or misleading descriptions
- Cameras used for harassment, stalking, or intimidation
- Cameras hidden or disguised to deceive
- Doxxing (exposing personal information)

**‚úó Illegal Content**
- Child exploitation material (automatic report to NCMEC)
- Voyeurism or "upskirt" cameras
- Recording in places with reasonable expectation of privacy
- Content that violates any law

**‚úó Inappropriate Focus**
- Cameras primarily focused on minors (schools, playgrounds) without proper authorization
- Cameras positioned to invade privacy (zoomed into windows)
- Sexualized or pornographic content
- Graphic violence or illegal activities

**‚úó Commercial Misuse**
- Unauthorized surveillance of competitors
- Corporate espionage
- Spam or advertising without permission
- Scraping data for commercial purposes

## üîä Audio Recording

### Special Considerations

If your camera records audio:
- **Know your state's laws** (some require two-party consent)
- **Post visible signage** if recording in public business areas
- **Disable audio for public streams** if legally uncertain
- **Never record private conversations** without all parties' consent

**Tip:** When in doubt, disable audio for public cameras. Video-only is safer legally.

## üë• Privacy and Respect

### Respect Your Neighbors

**DO:**
- Point cameras at your own property
- Angle cameras to avoid neighbors' windows and yards
- Inform neighbors if your camera may incidentally capture their property
- Respond politely to privacy concerns

**DON'T:**
- Intentionally surveil neighbors
- Share cameras that make others uncomfortable
- Ignore reasonable privacy requests
- Use cameras for disputes or harassment

### Expectation of Privacy

Even in public spaces, respect reasonable expectations:
- Don't zoom into private areas
- Avoid focusing on individuals without cause
- Be thoughtful about vulnerable locations (women's shelters, domestic violence safe houses)

## üö∏ Children's Privacy

### Special Protections

**We prohibit:**
- Cameras with children as primary subjects
- School cameras (unless official school property with authorization)
- Playground cameras in residential areas
- Any camera used to surveil or exploit minors

**Exceptions:**
- Your own children in your own yard (still: don't share publicly)
- Public events where children are incidentally present (parades, fairs)
- Official educational/institutional cameras with proper authorization

**If children appear in frame incidentally** (passing by on sidewalk), that's generally acceptable if camera's purpose is legitimate (traffic, weather).

## üé• Technical Requirements

### Camera Quality and Setup

**Required:**
- Camera must be reasonably stable (not constant shaking)
- Image must be clear enough to be useful
- Camera must be online consistently (>80% uptime preferred)
- Provide working stream (not 24/7 error screen)

**Recommended:**
- 720p or higher resolution
- Weatherproof for outdoor cameras
- Night vision if 24/7 operation
- Motion detection to reduce bandwidth

## üö® Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you see a camera that violates these guidelines:

**Report Button:**
1. Click "Report" on the camera page
2. Select reason (privacy violation, inappropriate content, fake location, etc.)
3. Provide brief description
4. Submit anonymously or with your account

**We will:**
- Review within 24 hours
- Remove violating cameras immediately if serious
- Request clarification from owner if borderline
- Notify you of outcome (if you provided email)

### What Happens to Violators

**First offense (minor):**
- Warning + request to fix issue
- Camera temporarily removed until compliant

**First offense (serious):**
- Immediate camera removal
- Account suspension pending review

**Repeat offenses:**
- Permanent ban from sharing public cameras
- Account termination

**Illegal content:**
- Immediate report to law enforcement (NCMEC for child exploitation)
- Account termination
- Cooperation with authorities

## ‚öñÔ∏è Legal Compliance

### Your Responsibility

**You must:**
- Comply with all federal, state, and local laws
- Understand surveillance and recording laws in your jurisdiction
- Obtain necessary permits if required
- Respect copyright (music, TV shows captured incidentally)
- Honor DMCA takedown requests

**Common Legal Issues:**

**Wiretapping Laws:**
- Federal: One-party consent for audio recording
- State: Some require two-party consent (CA, FL, PA, MD, etc.)
- Solution: Disable audio or post clear warning signs

**Voyeurism Laws:**
- All states prohibit recording in places with reasonable expectation of privacy
- Penalties include criminal charges

**Harassment Laws:**
- Using cameras to harass or intimidate is illegal
- Stalking via camera can result in restraining orders or charges

**Disclaimer:** These guidelines are not legal advice. Consult an attorney if unsure about your specific situation.
